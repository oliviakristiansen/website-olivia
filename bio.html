olivia lemmelin

biography

Olivia Lemmelin began her music studies at a young age, violin at 4 and piano at 6. As a child, she had the privilege of attending Suzuki programs at the Colburn School of Music in Los Angeles and the Hartt School of Music in Hartford, Connecticut. After moving to Idaho, she studied with Jeanette Kurtz (violin) and then Craig Purdy (violin), Assistant Professor of violin at Boise State University during high school. She then attended the Indiana University Summer Strings program for violin in 2007. In November of 2008, she was chosen to be the Idaho representative for violin in the MTNA Competition held in Spokane, WA. In 2009, Olivia was the Senior Strings winner of the Meridian Symphony Young Artist Competition, as well as placing first in the Idaho State Solo Contest. She was Concertmaster with the Boise Philharmonic Youth Orchestra and was chosen to participate in the All-Northwest Orchestra in Spokane, Washington. That fall, she began her studies with Danwen Jiang, Assistant Professor of Violin at Arizona State University after receiving Arizona State University’s Special Talents Award/Scholarship. During that time she was a hard working student participating in as many ensembles as she could including quintets, quartets, and trios and was fortunate to work with many professional quartets such as St. Lawrence String Quartet, Brentano Quartet, and the Shanghai Quartet, along with many others. In May 2013, Olivia earned her Bachelor of Music Performance Degree in Violin Performance. She again received the Special Talents Award and finished her Master’s Degree in Violin Performance at ASU in December 2015.
  Olivia is also an accomplished pianist and was accepted to several schools for a Piano Performance Bachelor’s degree before deciding to study violin. She studied with Dr. Del Parkinson of Boise State University and the Boise Philharmonic throughout high school. Under his instruction, she won many different piano festivals and competitions, always receiving superior ratings.
  Throughout the past six years, Olivia has performed with/in a wide variety of venues. She is the founding violinist of Simply Three, a popular violin/cello/bass trio, playing with them for three years. She enjoys playing in pit orchestras for musicals and operas, and classical ensembles, including the McCall Summer Musicfest orchestra. In addition, She has participated in the Manchester Music Festival in Manchester, Vermont and the InterHarmony International Music Festival in Sulzbach-Rosenberg, Germany. She is also one of the violinists for The Brilliance and she has enjoyed recording and touring with them throughout the United States. In February of 2015 she competed and won Grand Champion in the Vivaldi concerto competition with “Winter” put on by the Four Seasons Orchestra and performed with them in October of the same year. During her Master’s Degree, she was concertmaster and principle second several times for the ASU symphony and had the wonderful opportunity to work with Maestro Carl St. Clair (Music Director of Pacific Symphony) and Karina Canellakis (Assistant Conductor of the Dallas Symphony). She also performed Schulhoff Conzert for String Quartet and Wind Orchestra as first violin in November of 2015.
  Olivia is enjoying a career in performance, both on stage and in the recording studio and currently teaches private violin, viola and piano lessons. She recently has begun a career in Web Development designing apps and websites. She attended Coder Camps learning JavaScript and currently is working there as a mentor for the online JavaScript class.
